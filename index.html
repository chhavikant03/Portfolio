About Me

I'm Chhavi Kant, a final-year Computer Engineering student from Faridabad with a deep curiosity about how products shape human behavior. I got hooked on product thinking when I realized that great products aren't just about code—they're about solving real problems for real people. Coming from an engineering background, I've learned to love the intersection of technical feasibility and user empathy. I'm driven by the question: "What if we could make this experience 10x better?"

I believe the best product decisions come from listening—to users, to data, to your team's diverse perspectives. I'm someone who asks "why" before jumping to solutions, loves whiteboarding with cross-functional teams, and gets genuinely excited about uncovering insights from user research. I think collaboration isn't just nice-to-have; it's how you avoid building features nobody wants.

I'm looking for my first role at a company where I can grow as a product thinker while making a real impact. I'm drawn to mission-driven organizations, early-stage problems, and teams that value curiosity as much as execution. Whether it's a structured APM program or a BA role with product potential, I'm ready to learn fast and contribute meaningfully.
